Who Am I?

About Me

My name is Orlando Saldaña, I am a Computer Engineering student at the University of Puerto Rico Mayagüez. My interests are hardware engineering and embedded systems.

From a young age I had an interst in computers and how they worked. I was always interested in opening up laptops and gaming consoles when they stopped working (even though I did not know how they worked) and tried to fix them. In my high school years I started developing an interest in programming, specifically web development which gave me my introduction into the programming world. In 2016 I had the opportunity to join the Arecibo Observatory Space Academy (AOSA) which was a research experience for junior and senior high school students. In AOSA I found my true calling, hardware engineering. During my time at AOSA I worked with a team of like-minded students to develop an energy harvesting tile system for the visitor center at the observatory. Here I discovered electrical and computer engineering and knew what I needed to do to continue my education. In college I continued my work on hardware engineering and specifically embedded systems where I worked with a team of students developing Internet of Things devices such as an automatic pill dispenser which responded to commands through Google Assistant. I later worked in collaboration with the University of Texas El Paso to kick start the development of an IoT device capable of detecting power grid activity during natural disaster to improve the resiliency of the grid.
